# 🎨 Les Adjectifs (Adjectives)

Adjectives describe nouns. In French, they must agree with the noun in **gender** (masculine/feminine) and **number** (singular/plural).

## 1. L'Accord des Adjectifs (Agreement)

| Gender | Rule | Example (M -> F) | Prononciation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Général** | Add **-e** | grand -> grande | [ɡʁɑ̃] -> [ɡʁɑ̃d] |
| **Ends in -e** | No change | calme -> calme | [kalm] |
| **Ends in -eux** | Becomes **-euse** | heureux -> heureuse | [œʁø] -> [œʁøz] |
| **Ends in -f** | Becomes **-ve** | sportif -> sportive | [spɔʁtif] -> [spɔʁtiv] |
| **Ends in -el** | Becomes **-elle** | cruel -> cruelle | [kʁɥɛl] |

**Plural:** Usually add **-s** (silent). If it already ends in **-s** or **-x**, no change (*heureux* stays *heureux*).

---

## 2. La Place des Adjectifs (Placement)

Most adjectives go **AFTER** the noun.
- *Example:* Un livre **intéressant** [œ̃ livʁ ɛ̃teʁɛsɑ̃].

### ⚠️ The BAGS Rule (Before the noun)
Small, common adjectives go **BEFORE** the noun. Remember the acronym **BAGS**:
- **B**eauty: *beau* [bo], *joli* [ʒɔli]
- **A**ge: *vieux* [vjø], *jeune* [ʒœn], *nouveau* [nuvo]
- **G**oodness: *bon* [bɔ̃], *mauvais* [mɔvɛ], *meilleur* [mɛjœʁ]
- **S**ize: *petit* [pəti], *grand* [ɡʁɑ̃], *gros* [ɡʁo]

---

## 3. Adjectives that change meaning
Some adjectives change meaning depending on if they are before or after the noun.

| Adjective | Before Noun | After Noun |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ancien** | former | old/ancient |
| **cher** | dear (friend) | expensive |
| **propre** | own | clean |
| **grand** | great (man) | tall |
