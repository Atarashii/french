# 🗣️ Prononciation et Phonétique (Basics)

French pronunciation is often different from English because many letters are **silent**.

## 1. The "Silent" Final Consonants
As a general rule, if a word ends in **D, P, S, T, X, or Z**, the letter is **Silent**.
- *Example:* "Parler" ➔ "Parl-ay". "Paris" ➔ "Par-ee". "Salut" ➔ "Sal-u".

## 2. The Letter "H" (Silent India)
In French, the letter **H** is **always silent**.
- *Example:* "Hôtel" ➔ "O-tel". "Hôpital" ➔ "O-pi-tal".

## 3. The Sound of "G"
The letter **G** changes its sound based on the letter that follows it:
- **Hard "G"** (like "Goat"): Before **A, O, U**.
  - *Example:* **Ga**re, **Go**mme, **Gu**itare.
- **Soft "J"** (like "Measure"): Before **E, I, Y**.
  - *Example:* **Ge**nou, **Gi**rafe.

## 4. Liaisons (Linking Sounds)
When a word ends in a silent consonant (like S) and the next word starts with a **Vowel**, you "link" them together.
- **S + Vowel** ➔ Sounds like a **"Z"**.
  - *Example:* "Les amis" ➔ "Lay-Zami".
- **G + Vowel** ➔ Sometimes results in a harder link or specific phonetic shift depending on the context.
