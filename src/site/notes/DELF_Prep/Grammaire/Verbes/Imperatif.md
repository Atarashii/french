# 🗣️ L'Impératif (The Imperative)

The imperative is used to give **orders**, **advice**, or **requests**. It only exists for three persons: **Tu**, **Nous**, and **Vous**.

## 1. How to form it?
It is based on the **Present Tense** conjugation, but the subject pronoun (*Tu, Nous, Vous*) is **removed**.

### 🧱 Regular Verbs
| Subject | Formation Rule | Example (Parler) | Example (Finir) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tu** | Present form, **drop the -s** for -ER verbs | **Parle !** (Speak!) | **Finis !** (Finish!) |
| **Nous** | Same as Present | **Parlons !** (Let's speak!) | **Finissons !** (Let's finish!) |
| **Vous** | Same as Present | **Parlez !** (Speak! - plural/formal) | **Finissez !** (Finish!) |

### ⚠️ The "S" Exception (The "Tu" form)
For verbs ending in **-ER** (like *manger, aller*), you **drop the "s"** in the *Tu* form.
- *English:* "Eat your pizza!" ➔ *French:* "**Mange** ta pizza !" (Not *Manges*).
- **Trick:** If the imperative is followed by **y** or **en**, the "s" comes back for pronunciation: **Vas-y !** (Go ahead!).

---

## 2. Irregular Verbs (The Big 4)
These do not follow the present tense rules and must be memorized.

| Verb | Tu | Nous | Vous |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Être** (to be) | **Sois** | Soyons | Soyez |
| **Avoir** (to have) | **Aie** | Ayons | Ayez |
| **Savoir** (to know) | **Sache** | Sachons | Sachez |
| **Vouloir** (to want) | **Veuille** | - | Veuillez |

---

## 3. Reflexive Verbs (Pronominal)
In a positive command, the pronoun goes **after** the verb with a hyphen. **Te** becomes **Toi**.
- *Example:* "Get up!" ➔ "**Lève-toi !**"
- *Example:* "Let's get up!" ➔ "**Levons-nous !**"

In a negative command (prohibition), the pronoun stays **before** the verb.
- *Example:* "Don't get up!" ➔ "**Ne te lève pas !**"

---

## 🚫 Negative Imperative
Simply wrap the imperative verb with **Ne... pas**.
- *Example:* "**Ne mange pas** ça !" (Don't eat that!)
- *Example:* "**Ne parlons pas** maintenant." (Let's not talk now.)

## 💡 Exam Tip: "The Polite Request"
Use the **Vous** form to sound polite, even in exams.
- "Asseyez-vous, s'il vous plaît" (Please sit down).
- "Attendez un instant" (Wait a moment).
