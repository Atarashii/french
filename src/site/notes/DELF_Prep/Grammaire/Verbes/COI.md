# 🔗 Les Pronoms COI (Indirect Object Pronouns)

COI stands for **Complément d'Objet Indirect**. These pronouns replace a person preceded by the preposition "**à**".

## 1. List of COI Pronouns

| Person | Pronoun | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Je** | **me** | (to) me |
| **Tu** | **te** | (to) you |
| **Il / Elle** | **lui** | (to) him / her |
| **Nous** | **nous** | (to) us |
| **Vous** | **vous** | (to) you (plural/formal) |
| **Ils / Elles** | **leur** | (to) them |

---

## 2. When to use them?
You use a COI when the verb is followed by **à + [person]**.
- *Example:* Parler **à** quelqu'un (To speak to someone).
- *Example:* Téléphoner **à** quelqu'un (To call someone).
- *Example:* Envoyer **à** quelqu'un (To send to someone).

### 🧱 Examples
- Je parle **à Mirantsoa**. ➔ Je **lui** parle. (I speak to her).
- J'envoie un mail **à mes amis**. ➔ Je **leur** envoie un mail. (I send them an email).
- Elle **me** téléphone. (She calls me).

---

## 3. Position in the sentence
In most cases, the pronoun goes **BEFORE** the conjugated verb.

- **Simple Tense:** Je **lui** parle.
- **Passé Composé:** Je **lui** ai parlé. (Between subject and auxiliary).
- **Near Future:** Je vais **lui** parler. (Between "aller" and the infinitive).
- **Negation:** Je ne **lui** parle pas.

---

## 4. Common COI Verbs (A2)
- **parler à** (to speak to)
- **écrire à** (to write to)
- **répondre à** (to answer to)
- **dire à** (to say to)
- **donner à** (to give to)
- **offrir à** (to offer to)
- **demander à** (to ask to)
- **téléphoner à** (to call)
- **envoyer à** (to send to)
