# ⚙️ Conjugaison : Le Présent (Verb Endings)

In English, we only change the verb once (I eat, he eat**s**). In French, the **ending** of the verb changes for *every* subject (I, you, he, we, etc.).

## 1. What is "Conjugation"?
Think of it like a **Uniform**. Each subject has its own "hat" (ending) that the verb must wear.

### 🧱 The Most Common Uniform (Type -ER)
90% of French verbs end in **-er** (e.g., *manger, parler, regarder*). To conjugate them, you "cut off" the **-er** and add the "uniform" for that person.

| Subject | Uniform (Ending) | Example (Parler - To Speak) | Pronunciation Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Je** (I) | **-e** | Je parl**e** [paʁl] | (Silent) |
| **Tu** (You) | **-es** | Tu parl**es** [paʁl] | (Silent) |
| **Il / Elle / On** (He/She) | **-e** | Il parl**e** [paʁl] | (Silent) |
| **Nous** (We) | **-ons** | Nous parl**ons** [paʁlɔ̃] | "On" (Nasally) |
| **Vous** (You/Plural) | **-ez** | Vous parl**ez** [paʁle] | "Ay" |
| **Ils / Elles** (They) | **-ent** | Ils parl**ent** [paʁl] | (Silent!) |

## 2. The 4 Pillars (Irregular Verbs)
These are essential for DELF A2. You must know them by heart.

| Subject | Être [ɛtʁ] (to be) | Avoir [avwaʁ] (to have) | Aller [ale] (to go) | Venir [vəniʁ] (to come) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Je** | suis [sɥi] | ai [e] | vais [vɛ] | viens [vjɛ̃] |
| **Tu** | es [ɛ] | as [a] | vas [va] | viens [vjɛ̃] |
| **Il / Elle** | est [ɛ] | a [a] | va [va] | vient [vjɛ̃] |
| **Nous** | sommes [sɔm] | avons [avɔ̃] | allons [alɔ̃] | venons [vənɔ̃] |
| **Vous** | êtes [ɛt] | avez [ave] | allez [ale] | venez [vəne] |
| **Ils / Elles** | sont [sɔ̃] | ont [ɔ̃] | vont [vɔ̃] | viennent [vjɛn] |

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition for Exams
If you see a sentence and don't know the word, look at the **Ending**:
1.  **-ons** ➔ Always **Nous** (We).
2.  **-ez** ➔ Always **Vous** (You).
3.  **-ent** ➔ Always **Ils/Elles** (They).
4.  **-es** ➔ Almost always **Tu** (You).
