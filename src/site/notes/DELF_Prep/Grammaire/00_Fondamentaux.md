# 🗝️ Les Fondamentaux : Grammar for Beginners

If you find grammar theory confusing, think of it like a **LEGO set**. You have different types of "bricks," and they only fit together in specific ways.

## 1. The Building Blocks (Parts of Speech)

### 🧱 The Noun (Le Nom)
- **What it is:** A person, place, thing, or idea (*Joe, Paris, Pizza, Happiness*).
- **French Rule:** Every noun has a **Gender** (Masculine or Feminine). You must learn the gender *with* the word (e.g., don't just learn "café," learn "le café").

### 🧱 The Article (L'Article)
- **What it is:** The "little word" before the noun (*the, a, some*).
- **French Rule:** You **cannot** leave a noun "naked" in French. You almost always need an article.
  - *English:* "I like pizza."
  - *French:* "J'aime **la** pizza." (You must use "the" even for general things).

### 🧱 The Verb (Le Verbe)
- **What it is:** The "Action" or "State of Being" (*to eat, to be, to go*).
- **French Rule:** Verbs change their endings (**Conjugation**) based on *who* is doing the action.
  - *English:* I eat, you eat, he eat**s**. (Only 1 change).
  - *French:* Je mang**e**, Tu mang**es**, Nous mang**eons**. (6 different endings!).

### 🧱 The Subject (Le Sujet)
- **What it is:** The person/thing doing the action (*I, You, He/She, We*).
- **French Rule:** You must always include the subject (Je, Tu, Il...) unless you are giving a command.

---

## 2. The Timeline (Tenses)

| Tense | English Equivalent | When to use it? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Présent** | I eat / I am eating | Right now, or things that are always true. |
| **Passé Composé** | I ate / I have eaten | A specific action that **started and finished** (like a snapshot). |
| **Imparfait** | I used to eat / I was eating | Background info, habits, or feelings in the past (like a movie). |
| **Futur Proche** | I am going to eat | Something you are about to do or are certain will happen. |

---

## 📝 Exam Tip: "The Trimming Method"
When writing an exam, look for the **Subject** first. If the subject is "Nous," the verb *almost always* ends in **-ons**. If the answer choices don't end in -ons, cross them out immediately!
