# ⚡ Les Adverbes (Adverbs)

Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. Unlike adjectives, they **never change** (no agreement).

## 1. Formation des Adverbes

Most adverbs end in **-ment** (equivalent to English *-ly*).

| Adjective Type | Rule | Example | Prononciation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Feminine Adj** | Add **-ment** | heureuse -> heureusement | [œʁøzmɑ̃] |
| **Ends in Vowel** | Add **-ment** to masc. | vrai -> vraiment | [vʁɛmɑ̃] |
| **Ends in -ant** | Becomes **-amment** | courant -> couramment | [kuʁamɑ̃] |
| **Ends in -ent** | Becomes **-emment** | prudent -> prudemment | [pʁydamɑ̃] |

### ⚠️ Irregulars (Common in A2)
- **bon** (good) -> **bien** [bjɛ̃] (well)
- **mauvais** (bad) -> **mal** [mal] (badly)
- **gentil** (kind) -> **gentiment** [ʒɑ̃timɑ̃] (kindly)
- **meilleur** (better adj) -> **mieux** [mjø] (better adv)

---

## 2. La Place de l'Adverbe (Placement)

### 🧱 Simple Tenses (Present, Imparfait)
The adverb goes **AFTER** the conjugated verb.
- *Example:* Je parle **lentement** [ʒə paʁl lɑ̃tmɑ̃].

### 🧱 Compound Tenses (Passé Composé)
- **Long adverbs** go AFTER the past participle (*Je suis arrivé heureusement*).
- **Short/Common adverbs** go **BETWEEN** the auxiliary and the participle.
  - *Keywords:* **bien, mal, déjà, souvent, toujours, trop, beaucoup**.
  - *Example:* J'ai **bien** mangé [ʒe bjɛ̃ mɑ̃ʒe].

---

## 3. Interaction avec les Adjectifs (Modifying Adjectives)

When an adverb modifies an adjective, it is placed **directly BEFORE** the adjective.

### 🧱 Examples with BAGS Adjectives (Before Noun)
- Un **très beau** jour [œ̃ tʁɛ bo ʒuʁ]. (A very beautiful day).
- Une **vraiment petite** maison [yn vʁɛmɑ̃ pətit mɛzɔ̃]. (A really small house).

### 🧱 Examples with Regular Adjectives (After Noun)
- Un livre **extrêmement intéressant** [œ̃ livʁ ɛkstʁɛmmɑ̃ ɛ̃teʁɛsɑ̃]. (An extremely interesting book).
- Un homme **vraiment gentil** [œ̃n ɔm vʁɛmɑ̃ ʒɑ̃ti]. (A really kind man).
