# 🍎 L'Article Partitif (The Partitive Article)

The partitive article is used to express an **unknown or uncountable quantity** of something (like "some" or "any" in English).

## 1. When to use it?
Used for things you cannot count individually (liquids, mass, abstract concepts) or when you don't know the exact amount.
- *English:* "I want some bread." ➔ *French:* "Je veux **du** pain." [ʒə vø dy pɛ̃]
- *English:* "She has courage." ➔ *French:* "Elle a **du** courage." [ɛl a dy kuʁaʒ]

## 📊 Formation Table
| Gender | Singular | Before Vowel/H | Pluriel |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Masculin** | **du** [dy] (de + le) | **de l'** [də l] | **des** [de] (de + les) |
| **Féminin** | **de la** [də la] | **de l'** [də l] | **des** [de] (de + les) |

### 🧱 Examples
- **Du** café [dy kafe] (Some coffee - M)
- **De la** soupe [də la sup] (Some soup - F)
- **De l'** eau [də lo] (Some water - Vowel)
- **Des** épinards [dez epinaʁ] (Some spinach - Plural)

---

## 2. The Big "DE" Rule (Negation & Quantity)

### 🚫 In Negation
When the sentence is negative, **un, une, des, du, de la** all turn into **DE** (or **D'**).
- *Positive:* "J'ai **un** chien." ➔ *Negative:* "Je n'ai pas **de** chien."
- *Positive:* "J'ai **de la** patience." [ʒe də la pasjɑ̃s] ➔ *Negative:* "Je n'ai pas **de** patience." [ʒə ne pa də pasjɑ̃s]
- *Positive:* "Tu fais **du** sport." ➔ *Negative:* "Tu ne fais pas **de** sport."
- *Rule:* **Un / Une / Des / Du / De la** ➔ **DE** after negation.

### ⚖️ With Quantities
After a word that expresses a specific quantity (un peu, beaucoup, un kilo), always use **DE**.
- *Example:* "Beaucoup **de** vin" [boku də vɛ̃] (A lot of wine).
- *Example:* "Un peu **de** confort" [œ̃ pø də kɔ̃fɔʁ] (A little comfort).

---

## 3. Sports & Instruments
We use the partitive with the verb **FAIRE** for sports and **JOUER** for instruments.
- "Je fais **du** foot." [ʒə fɛ dy fut] (I play soccer).
- "Elle joue **de la** guitare." [ɛl ʒu də la ɡitaʁ] (She plays guitar).

## 💡 Tip for Remembering
If you can translate it as "**some**" in English, you probably need a partitive in French. 
*Remember:* 
- **Le/La** = The (Specific/General preference like "J'aime le café").
- **Un/Une** = One/A (Countable).
- **Du/De la** = Some (Uncountable).
