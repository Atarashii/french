# 📚 Les Articles & Prépositions (English Guide)

In English, we are often "lazy" with articles (the, a, some). In French, the rules are very strict because the article tells you if the noun is **Male (M)** or **Female (F)**.

## 1. Why do we need articles?
In French, you **cannot** have a noun alone after a verb. 
- **Rule of Thumb:** If you say a noun, put a "little word" (Article, Possessive, or Preposition) in front of it.
- **Example:** "I drink milk" ➔ "Je bois **du** lait" (literally: "I drink *some* milk").

## 📊 The Article Map

| Type | When to use it? | Masculin | Féminin | Pluriel | Before Vowel (a, e, i, o, u, y, h) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Défini** (The) | Specific things you already know. | **le** | **la** | **les** | **l'** |
| **Indéfini** (A/An/Some) | General things you don't know yet. | **un** | **une** | **des** | - |
| **Partitif** (Some) | Uncountable things (liquid, mass). | **du** | **de la** | **des** | **de l'** |

### ⚠️ Exam Tip: "The S-Rule"
- If a noun ends in **S** or **X**, it is **Plural**.
- In this case, use **les** or **des**.
- This is an easy point in the exam!

---

## 🔗 The "Fusion" (Contracted Prepositions)
French does not like the sound of "à le" or "de le." They "fuse" into a new word.

| English | Wrong French | Correct French | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| To the (M) | à le | **au** | *à* + *le* = *au* (Sounds better). |
| To the (Plural) | à les | **aux** | *à* + *les* = *aux*. |
| Of the (M) | de le | **du** | *de* + *le* = *du*. |
| Of the (Plural) | de les | **des** | *de* + *les* = *des*. |

## 📝 Practice Sentence
- *English:* "I am going to the (M) restaurant with some (Pl) friends."
- *French:* "Je vais **au** restaurant avec **des** amis."
